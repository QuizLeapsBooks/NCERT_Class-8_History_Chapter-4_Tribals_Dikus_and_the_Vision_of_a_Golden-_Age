Level-2 Case Study

Passage 1

Some of them practised jhum cultivation, that is, shifting cultivation. This was done on small patches of land, mostly in forests. The cultivaters cut the tree tops to allow sunlight to reach the and burnt the vegetation on the land to clear it for cultivation. They spread ash from firing, which contain potash, to fertile the soil. They used the axe to cut trees and hoe to scratch the soil in order to preapare it for cultivation. They brodcast the seeds, that is, scattered the seed on the field instead of ploughing the land sowing the seeds. Once the crop was ready and harvested, they move to another field. A field that had been cultivated once was left for several years.
